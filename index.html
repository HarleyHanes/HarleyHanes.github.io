Welcome!

Thank you for visiting my webpage. I am an applied mathematician begining his career in computational analysis of disease transmission models, particularly through uncertainty quantification and sensitivity analysis. I am passionate about identifying human risk for zoonotic infectious diseases, especially those involving non-human mammalian hosts such as Chagas disease, Lyme disease, and viral hemorrhagic fevers. I hope to use my skills in modeling and computational analysis to make a career assessing infectious disease risk and providing public health professionals insight on life saving interventions and control protocols.

I am currently studying for my master's in Computational Science at Tulane University with Dr. James (Mac) Hyman , searching for summer internships at national labs, and applying to Applied Math PhD programs.
